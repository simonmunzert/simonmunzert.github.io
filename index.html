Current Research

My research focuses on the measurement of public opinion, political representation, and the use of new media in society. Methodologically, I follow new developments in survey research and data collection as well as hierarchical modeling and Bayesian statistics.

Currently, I am involved in three major projects:

  • Media Exposure and Opinion Formation in an Age of Information Overload (joint project with Pablo Barberá, Andrew Guess and JungHwan Yang, funded by the Volkswagen Foundation)
  • Forecasting the 2017 German Federal Election (joint project with Thomas Gschwend, Marcel Neunhoeffer, Sebastian Sternberg, Lukas Stoetzer and Steffen Zittlau)
  • Citizen and Elite Activity on the Wikipedia Market Place of Political Information (funded by the Daimler and Benz Foundation)
